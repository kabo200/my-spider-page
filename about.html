Venomous Spiders

Black Widow

Recognized by its shiny black body and red hourglass mark. It has strong venom but rarely bites unless disturbed.

Brown Recluse

Light brown spider with a violin-shaped marking. Its bite may damage skin tissue in severe cases.

Sydney Funnel Web

Highly venomous spider from Australia. Often found in moist sheltered places like gardens and forests.

Brazilian Wandering Spider

A fast active hunter that does not build webs. Known for defensive behavior and powerful venom.

Spider Facts & Knowledge

What Are Spiders?

Spiders are arachnids with eight legs and two body parts. They help control insects by feeding on them.

Where Do Spiders Live?

Spiders live in forests, grasslands, deserts, gardens, and homes across the world.

Dangers of Spiders

Most spiders are harmless, but some venomous species may cause serious medical reactions.

Interesting Facts

There are over 48,000 known spider species and they are found on every continent except Antarctica.

Did You Know?

Some spiders can live for several years and large species may have a leg span of 30 cm.

Safety Tips

Stay calm when encountering spiders. Avoid touching them and contact pest professionals if needed.
